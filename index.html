Day 1

You find yourself sitting in an office, shadowed by the light of an endless amount of souls. Each one of them hovers and floats around you, their stories reduced and condensed into a single sheet of paper, all stacked neatly on the side of your desk. On the papers are only a brief description, their virtues, their sins, and the decision: heaven or hell.

The fates of the souls are in your hands.


Aaron Coello

Species: Human Gender: Male
Age: 9
Cause of death: Hit by a train

Lived in an orphanage where he was outcasted by the other children. His only friend was a rabbit plush, and he was always kind to people close to him. Held deep resentment for the kids who made fun of him and often wished misfortune upon them.


Heaven Hell

Elio Rowling

Species: Witch, vampire
Gender: Male
Age: 1528
Cause of death: Cursed artifact

Trafficked and experimented on many children under the guise of an orphanage. Wanted to achieve immortality through his experiments but ended up gaining it after being turned into a vampire. Discovered an artifact and used it without knowing about the curse it contained.


Heaven Hell

Calvin Sova

Species: Avian, Owl
Gender: Male
Age: 25
Cause of death: Killed in a war

Grew up in a family of nobility and became a knight for his kingdom. Went through many misfortunes in his lifetime because of his “bad luck”. Tried to protect his people until the end, and though he was a knight, has never killed a soul.


Heaven Hell

Heaven. Hell. Hell. Heaven. The choices continue.

It keeps going on and on. An endless cycle. The names start to blur into one another and everything starts to look monotonous. Black and white. But you have to continue.

It's your job. Your duty. Your purpose.

Your name is Azariel Dalvir, a reaper who decides the fates of souls with an impartial judgement. The gods have placed their faith in you, and you need to carry out their will.

But as the hours stretch longer, a thought crosses your mind. A question that had always been in the corner of your mind, one that you could never dare to voice.

Is this all there is?

Choice 1: Bare with the boredom and continue working.
Choice 2: Take a break and reflect for a moment.

Bare with the boredom and continue working.

You scratch that thought out of your head. It’s not the time to be doubting yourself.

There’s still work to do. Souls to judge.

You can’t stop now.

...

The day ends as it always does. You continue making decisions until your shift is over. Until the last soul is sorted.

You stare at the paperwork on your desk. Profiles of the virtuous and the sinful neatly stacked in two piles in front of you.

Tonight, as you lay in bed, you think about the people you judged. Their lives, their goals, their stories—they must have been so fulfilling. Nothing like your own.

You suppress the thoughts for now. Tomorrow’s a new day, and you’ll need to continue your duty.

However, as you slowly drift to sleep, you dream of colours you’ve never seen before, sounds you’ve never heard, visions of people, and lives you could never live. As you continue to fall asleep, you wonder if you’ll ever be able to experience that someday…

You decide to take a break from working for a bit.

You stare at the soul profiles on ur desk. The descriptions of these people. They’re so short, lacking detail about their lives. You know that there are more to these stories. There must be.

Your mind starts drifting off. You think about the mortal realm, where these souls all come from. You can only imagine what it’s like—chaotic, unpredictable, alive. As an entity of the afterlife, a servant of the gods, you’ve never been allowed to leave this realm. But as these souls flow in, you keep wondering… what is it like?

What is it like to do whatever you wished?

What is it like to be unbound to these duties that keep you chained down at this desk?

What is it like to be…free?

It’s a dangerous thought to be having. Reapers aren’t allowed to stray from their station, and yet, as you continue dazing into space, you keep wondering.

What if you could?
